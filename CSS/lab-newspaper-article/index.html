City Launches Car-Free Sundays Across Downtown
Pilot aims to improve air quality, boost local business, and reclaim public space
By Davron Murodullaev

Crowds filled the boulevards as the city’s first car-free Sunday kicked off this morning. Families strolled, cyclists rang their bells, and street performers animated every corner. Early air-quality measurements showed a marked dip in particulates, according to officials.

Local cafés reported steady foot traffic and longer dwell times. Urban planners will analyze mobility data over the next six weeks to decide whether to expand the program citywide. Residents can submit feedback via the council’s participatory portal.
